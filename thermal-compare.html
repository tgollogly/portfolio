Thermal Compare

Case study

How it's built — and why

The problem

Comparing how hot several places actually feel means opening a weather tab for each and converting in your head. I wanted them side by side, ranked, in one view.

Stack & why I chose it

Plain vanilla JavaScript — no framework, because the scope is small and a framework would only add weight. Live data again from the key-free Open-Meteo API. The comparison bars are pure CSS, so there's no charting dependency.

Key decisions

Each city is fetched asynchronously with its own loading state, so one slow response doesn't block the others, and the list re-sorts live by apparent temperature as results arrive. Colour bands give an instant read of severity.

Trade-offs & what's next

Deliberately minimal — no map — to stay fast and focused. Next: shareable URLs that remember your cities, and historical comparison.

Data: open-meteo.com. Ranked by “feels-like” (apparent) temperature.
