Case study

How it's built — and why

The problem

Screening a company for possible earnings manipulation usually needs a forensic analyst. I wanted anyone to be able to run the recognised model in seconds.

Stack & why I chose it

This is pure client-side JavaScript with no backend, because the Beneish model is deterministic maths — so it runs instantly and, importantly, the financial figures never leave the browser, which is a real privacy advantage.

Key decisions

Rather than ask for pre-calculated ratios (which most people don't have), it takes raw figures for two years and computes all eight indices and the final M-score itself. The −1.78 threshold flags elevated risk. Two worked examples — a healthy and a red-flag company — are pre-loaded so it's usable and verifiable immediately.

Trade-offs & what's next

Figures are entered by hand rather than pulled from filings, to keep it dependency-free. Next: auto-fetching company data from the SEC EDGAR API.
